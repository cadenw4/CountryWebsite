Char siu, or Cantonese BBQ pork, is a beloved dish defined by its sweet, savory, and umami glaze coating tender. Char siu translated to “fork-roasted” is typically how it is made, usually marinated with honey, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and/or shaoxing wine. The pork, typically pork shoulder, is roasted on a skewer and glazed repeatedly to achieve the glossy, umami glaze. Char siu is typically served in a bun(char siu bao) or on rice (char siu fan).
