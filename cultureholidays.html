Language

In Hong Kong, the national language is recognized as Chinese (Cantonese and Mandarin ) and English. Cantonese is spoken by about 88% of the population while English is only spoken by around 5% of the population and Mandarin by about 2.6% of the population. While Cantonese and Mandarin are both considered Chinese, they both hold many differences as Cantonese has 6 tones compared to Mandarin with 4 tones. The writing system in both languages are the same, but Hong Kong does rely on Traditional Chinese writing while Mainland China uses the simplified Chinese characters.

Lunar New Year

Lunar New Year in Hong Kong is a vibrant, major festival featuring spectacular events like a Night Parade and Victoria Harbour Fireworks, temple visits (Wong Tai Sin, Che Kung), flower markets filled with auspicious blooms (orchids, kumquats), traditional foods (nian gao, turnip cake), lion dances, and family gatherings, all centered on bringing good fortune (red decorations, "fai chun") for the new lunar year, typically falling late Jan/Feb.

Dragon Boat Festival

The Dragon Boat Festival in Hong Kong, also known as the Tuen Ng Festival, is celebrated with races at locations like Stanley, Aberdeen, and Victoria Harbour.The festival honors the ancient Chinese poet Qu Yuan and the date varies, but is typically in mid-June. Beyond races, you can find carnivals with food and entertainment, and participate in traditions like making scented sachets.
