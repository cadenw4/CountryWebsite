Dim sum is a traditional Chinese meal of small, shareable plates (like dumplings, buns, rolls, meats, seafood) served with tea, often called yum cha ("drink tea") for its emphasis on tea and social gathering. Common dishes served in dim sum are: siu mai, xiaolongbao, har gao, chuengfun (steamed rice roll), lo mai gai (glutinous rice chicken), and pineapple buns. Most of these foods are cooked from steaming or baked.
